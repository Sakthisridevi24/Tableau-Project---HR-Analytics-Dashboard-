# 📊 HR Analytics Dashboard – Tableau Project

## 🔍 Overview
This HR Analytics Dashboard is an interactive Tableau project developed to analyze employee data and identify workforce trends such as attrition, job satisfaction, department-wise performance, age distribution, and gender-based insights.

The dashboard helps HR teams and management make data-driven decisions to improve employee retention and overall organizational performance.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze employee attrition trends
- Monitor workforce distribution by age and gender
- Evaluate department-wise attrition
- Understand job satisfaction across different job roles
- Identify education field-wise attrition trends

---

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies
- Tableau
- Data Visualization
- Data Analysis
- Dashboard Design

## 📊 Dashboard Features

### 🔹 Department Wise Attrition
Analyzes employee attrition across:
- HR
- R&D
- Sales

**Insight:**  
R&D department has the highest attrition compared to other departments.

---

### 🔹 Employee Distribution by Age Group
Displays employee count across different age categories.

**Insight:**  
Most employees belong to the 25–34 age group.

---

### 🔹 Job Satisfaction Rating
Shows satisfaction ratings for different job roles.

**Insight:**  
Research Scientists and Sales Executives have higher employee counts with varying satisfaction levels.

---

### 🔹 Education Field Wise Attrition
Analyzes attrition based on employees’ educational backgrounds.

**Insight:**  
Employees from Life Sciences and Medical fields show higher attrition.

---

### 🔹 Gender-Based Attrition by Age Group
Displays male and female attrition across age groups.

**Insight:**  
Male employee attrition is slightly higher than female attrition, especially in the 25–34 age category.

---

## 💡 Key Insights
- Overall attrition rate is 16%
- R&D department experiences the highest employee turnover
- Majority of employees are aged between 25–34
- Younger employees show higher attrition trends
- Job satisfaction varies across job roles
- Male attrition is higher compared to female employees
